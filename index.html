About Us

The Works Operations and Maintenance (WOM) division is responsible for the care of City roads, sidewalks, bridges, watercourses and storm sewers, as well as acquisition and servicing of most of the Cityâ€™s vehicles and equipment.

The division is comprised of staff spread across three sections:

  • Fleet Services
  • Technical Services
  • Works Administration, Operations and Maintenance

The Division maintains over 5,600 lane kilometers of road and 2,200 kilometers of sidewalk in Mississauga. In addition, there are more than 136 bridges and 119 culverts. The Works Administration, Operations and Maintenance section manages this critical infrastructure through comprehensive maintenance routines and standards of repair.

Municipalities have complete administrative control over highways/roads under the jurisdiction of the Municipal Act, and can choose to adopt the Minimum Maintenance Standards for Municipal Highways- Ontario Regulation 239/02 (MMSMH). However, in cases where the standards are not adopted by the municipality, judgements against such municipalities for poor maintenance will be made by the courts after comparison with the standards.

Not only do the MMSMH provide the City with some legal protection, they also provide a degree of uniformity and standardization of care on municipal roads that the travelling public depend upon, and which improves public safety.
